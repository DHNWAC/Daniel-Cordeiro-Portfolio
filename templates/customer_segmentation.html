Customer Segmentation Analysis

Identifying distinct customer groups to enable targeted marketing strategies and personalized experiences

Project Overview

This project conducts a comprehensive customer segmentation analysis for an e-commerce business. I have used advanced clustering techniques, RFM analysis and ML techniques.

Business Objectives

  • Identify distinct customer segments based on behavior and purchasing patterns
  • Develop targeted marketing strategies and personalized experiences for each segment
  • Create a recommendation system to predict products of interest for different customer groups
Technologies Used
Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn Scikit-learn TensorFlow Plotly
Techniques
RFM Analysis K-Means Clustering Neural Collaborative Filtering Cohort Analysis Customer Lifetime Value

Methodology

Analytical Approach
  1. RFM Analysis: Segmentation based on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value
  2. K-Means Clustering: Identifying natural customer groupings
  3. Neural Collaborative Filtering: Recommendation system using basic neural networks
  4. Cohort Analysis: Tracking customer retention and behavior over time
  5. Customer Lifetime Value: Predictive modelling of customer worth

Key Findings

1. RFM Analysis and Customer Clusters

Using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis combined with K-means clustering, I identified six distinct customer segments with unique behavioral patterns.

Customer Segments
Segment Description % of Customers
VIP Customers High frequency, high monetary value, recent purchases 15%
Loyal Customers Regular purchases with moderate value 22%
New Potential Recent first-time buyers with potential for growth 18%
Dormant High Value Previously valuable customers who haven't purchased recently 12%
Big Spenders Gone Cold High-value customers who have stopped purchasing 8%
Occasional Customers Infrequent purchases with low monetary value 25%

2. Neural Collaborative Filtering for Recommendations

I implemented a very basic neural network to reccommend personalised products. The model achieved a test RMSE of 0.86 which indicates strong performance.

4. Customer Lifetime Value Analysis

I developed a comprehensive CLV model that incorporates purchase frequency, average order value, and customer retention probability to estimate the future value of each customer segment.

Key CLV Insights
  • VIP customers represent 15% of the customer base but account for 42% of predicted future revenue
  • Reactivating dormant high-value customers offers significant ROI potential
  • New customers show promising lifetime value when effectively nurtured
  • Purchase frequency is the strongest predictor of high lifetime value
  • Customer acquisition cost should be differentiated by predicted segment CLV

Segment Strategies

VIP Customers

15% of customers, 42% of revenue

Characteristics
  • High purchase frequency (5+ orders/year)
  • High average order value ($120+)
  • Recent purchases (within 30 days)
  • Broad category exploration

Loyal Customers

22% of customers, 28% of revenue

Characteristics
  • Regular purchase frequency (3-4 orders/year)
  • Moderate average order value ($75-120)
  • Consistent category preferences
  • Price-sensitive but loyal

Dormant High Value

12% of customers, 8% of revenue

Characteristics
  • Previously frequent purchasers
  • No purchases in 60+ days
  • Historically high CLV
  • Often multi-category shoppers

Business Impact & Recommendations

Key Business Insights

  1. Segment-Based Strategy: The analysis revealed six distinct customer segments, each requiring different marketing approaches and retention strategies.
  2. High-Value Customer Focus: VIP and Loyal customers represent 37% of the customer base but account for 70% of revenue, justifying premium retention efforts.
  3. Reactivation Opportunity: Dormant high-value customers represent a $1.2M revenue opportunity if effectively reactivated.
  4. Product Associations: Strong product associations were identified that can drive bundling strategies and increase average order value.
  5. Customer Lifecycle Management: Cohort analysis revealed critical drop-off points where intervention is most effective.
