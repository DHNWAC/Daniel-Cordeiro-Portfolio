Causal Inference Analysis

Uncovering cause-effect relationships in socioeconomic data to inform policy decisions

Project Overview

I have completed some inference techniques in order to understand underlying relation ships between between education, income, health, and other factors.

Research Questions

  1. What is the true causal effect of education on income, controlling for confounding factors?
  2. How does education affect health outcomes, and what portion of this effect is mediated through income?
Technologies Used
Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn
Techniques
Causal Graphs Regression Analysis Mediation Analysis

Methodology

Analytical Approach
  1. Causal Model Specification: Developed a directed acyclic graph (DAG) to model assumed causal relationships
  2. Regression Analysis: Multiple regression models with carefully selected covariates to isolate causal effects
  3. Mediation Analysis: Quantified direct and indirect effects of education on health
  4. Machine Learning: Used Random Forest for feature importance and partial dependence plots
  5. Policy Simulation: Estimated potential outcomes under various intervention scenarios

Key Findings

1. Income Determinants

Each additional year of education increases income by approximately $3,000. This effect actually varies a great amount depending on the family background.

2. Mediation Analysis: Education → Income → Health

Education affects health both directly and indirectly through income. Approximately 42% of education's total effect on health is mediated through increased income.

Effect Type Magnitude Percentage
Total Effect 2.45 points 100%
Direct Effect 1.42 points 58%
Indirect Effect (via Income) 1.03 points 42%

Interpretation:

  • Each year of education directly increases health score by 1.42 points
  • Each year of education indirectly increases health score by 1.03 points through higher income
  • The significant mediation effect suggests that policies addressing both education and income will have the strongest impact on health outcomes

Conclusions

Key Insights

  1. Education Has Substantial Causal Effects: Each additional year of education increases income by approximately $3,000 after controlling for confounding factors.
  2. Health Effects Are Both Direct and Indirect: About 42% of education's effect on health is mediated through income, while 58% is direct (possibly through knowledge, behaviors, and access).
  3. Gender Wage Gap Increases with Age: The analysis revealed a significant interaction between gender and age, with the wage gap growing larger for older individuals.
